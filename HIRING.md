# Hiring Pipeline — Listing VAs

Owner-side doc. The funnel: **OLJ post → keyword filter → paid trial → hire → Telegram group.**
Nobody enters the Telegram group without passing a paid trial. No exceptions — that rule is
what keeps the group a roster of workers instead of an audience.

---

## 1. The OLJ job post (paste-ready)

Post under **Data Entry / Virtual Assistant**. Keep it always-on; never close it.

> **Title:** Product Listing VA — E-commerce (Long-term, Daily Work)
>
> **Job type:** Part-time or Full-time · **Salary:** ₱XX,XXX/month *(set before posting — see §4)*
>
> We run an e-commerce operation in Singapore. Your job: find products on Shopee
> Singapore using our simple rules, and post them to Carousell using titles and
> descriptions our app generates for you. No writing, no design, no customer
> chat — just careful product picking and clean posting.
>
> **You need:**
> - Your own laptop/PC and stable internet
> - Attention to detail — the job is following rules exactly, every time
> - Available daily (Mon–Sat)
> - Basic image editing (cropping, removing watermarks) — we'll show you how
>
> **You do NOT need:** experience with Carousell or Shopee. We have a full
> step-by-step guide and daily support.
>
> **Daily quota:** 100 listings/day once trained (paid training/ramp-up period).
>
> **How to apply:** Start your application with the word **MANGO** so we know
> you read this. Then answer:
> 1. What hours (Philippine time) can you work daily?
> 2. Describe a job where you had to follow detailed rules. What were the rules?
> 3. What is your expected monthly salary?
>
> Applications without MANGO or with copy-paste generic answers are not read.

---

## 2. Screening (15 min/week, batch it)

Once a week:
1. **Delete** everything not starting with MANGO (this is ~80%).
2. Skim the rest. Keep only: specific answers to Q2 (real detail, not "I am hardworking"),
   salary expectation inside your range, hours that overlap SG daytime.
3. Shortlist up to 5. Reply with the trial offer (§3). Don't interview — the trial
   is the interview.

## 3. The paid trial

Message to shortlisted applicants:

> Thanks for applying! Next step is a paid trial task so we can both see if the
> job fits. You'll get our SOP document. Task: source and fully prepare
> **20 listings** following the SOP exactly, within **48 hours** of starting.
> Pay: **₱XXX** via GCash on completion, whether or not we continue. Reply
> "READY" to receive the SOP and your work link.

- Give them a real worker link (`/va?w=...`) on the **trial/spare account** — never the main.
- Judge against the SOP rules they were given: category correct, SG seller, 4★+,
  price band, images cleaned, links pasted back. **Count rule violations, not speed.**
- 0–2 violations across 20 listings → hire. 3+ → pay the trial fee, pass politely.
- Ghosted or late without a reason → no pay owed, no reply needed.

## 4. Pay (fill in before first post)

| Item | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trial task (20 listings) | ₱XXX | One-time, GCash, pay same day they finish |
| Monthly salary | ₱XX,XXX | State it in the post — posts with salary get 3–5× more applicants |
| Referral bonus | ₱X,XXX | Paid ONLY after referred hire completes 30 days of real output |

Market reference (mid-2026): entry-level listing/data-entry VAs on OLJ typically
₱20,000–28,000/month full-time. Paying the top of the band filters better than
interviewing does.

## 5. After hiring

1. Send VA-SOP.md, confirm they've read it (ask one question only the SOP answers).
2. First week ramp: 30/day → 60/day → 100/day. Check their output daily during ramp.
3. **Then** invite to the Telegram group.
4. Tell them the referral deal (§4) — every good hire becomes a recruiter.

## 6. Standing rules

- The OLJ post never closes. Screen weekly even when not actively hiring —
  a bench of trial-passed people beats scrambling when someone quits.
- Trial-passers you don't need yet: tell them honestly ("you passed, next slot
  goes to you"), keep the list. That list IS the growth plan.
- Fire fast during ramp week. A bad fit at day 3 costs one week; at month 3 it
  costs the account.
