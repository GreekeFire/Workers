# Sales Playbook — chat, follow-up, upsell, fulfilment

Owner-side doc. VAs never touch sales (per SOP). Every buyer message gets handled
with the scripts below — no ad-libbing. Goal: direct, confident, never desperate.

---

## 1. The chat script

The flow is always: **confirm interest → ask for the order → answer questions →
payment.** Move to the next step the moment you can. Copy-paste and edit `[...]`.

**Buyer opens ("Hi, is this available?"):**
> Yes, still available 🙂 Would you like to order? Delivery is 2–3 working days.

**Buyer says yes / sounds keen:**
> Great — total is $[X], free delivery. You can PayNow to [number], or checkout
> with the Buy Button if you prefer buyer protection. Once payment's in I'll
> arrange delivery 🙂

**Buyer asks questions:** answer each one directly in one message, then re-ask:
> [answer]. Want me to arrange one for you?

**Buyer haggles ("can do $55?"):**
> Price is fixed at $[X] 🙂 already the lowest I can do.

**Buyer pushes again:** use the pull-back — release the pressure, don't chase:
> No worries if it's not within budget — no obligation at all. It'll be here if
> you change your mind 🙂

Then **stop messaging**. A real buyer very often comes back and pays full price.

**After payment received:**
> Received, thank you! Your [item] will arrive in 2–3 working days 🙂

### Hard rules
- **Never discount to close a haggler.** One concession invites three more.
- **Never promise a specific day or date.** Always a range: "2–3 working days."
  (A missed promised date is the #1 trigger for police reports → frozen bank account.)
- **One message, then wait.** Never double-text outside the follow-up schedule.
- **Never beg, plead, or chase.** Desperation kills the sale.

---

## 2. Follow-ups (3 days, then stop)

Not "are you still interested?" three times — each message gives a small reason
to reply. The real work is mechanical: any message bumps your chat to the top of
their inbox, where you're visible again. This is claimed to be worth 25–33% of
total profit.

**Day 1 (next day):**
> Hi! Just letting you know the [item] is still available if you'd like it 🙂

**Day 2:**
> Hi again — happy to arrange delivery this week if you're keen. Any questions
> I can answer?

**Day 3 (final):**
> Last check on the [item] — if I don't hear back I'll assume you're no longer
> keen. Thanks anyway! 🙂

After day 3: **give up and move on.** No exceptions — further messages read as
spam and can get you reported.

---

## 3. Upsell (after every sale)

Right after payment is confirmed, one message:

> Thanks for the order! 🙂 Since delivery's already arranged — anything else from
> my shop you'd like to add? I'll include a small free gift / take $5 off a
> second item if you add one before it ships: [link 1] [link 2]

- Send 1–3 links to **complementary items** from your own listings (bought a
  vacuum → offer the mop, the storage rack).
- Discounting HERE is fine — a $5 concession that creates a second $25-margin
  sale is +margin. Only haggle-discounts are banned.
- One offer only. If they decline or ignore, drop it.

---

## 4. CarouPay (Buy Button) fulfilment

When a buyer pays via the Buy Button instead of PayNow:

1. Profile page → red badge on the **My Sales** (star) tab = pending order.
2. Open the order → **Ship Order**.
3. Courier: scroll to **Other couriers** → type **"private courier"** (you won't
   have a tracking number — this is expected).
4. Confirm. Then actually place the Shopee order so the item ships.
5. Money releases when the buyer taps **Order Received**, or auto-releases after
   Carousell's holding period (~7 working days).

**Forgetting step 4 = buyer never gets the item = dispute.** Ship order in the
app AND order from Shopee in the same sitting.

---

## 5. Refunds — the clawback loop

Buyer unhappy / item defective / delivery too late:

1. **Refund the buyer immediately.** Never sit on their money — held funds are
   what turn complaints into police reports.
2. Get **evidence from the buyer first** (photos of defect etc.) — required for
   the Shopee claim.
3. Shopee/Lazada → Orders → **Return & Refund** → select the matching issue,
   attach the buyer's evidence.
4. If the platform can't refund outright: set the **pickup address to the
   buyer's address**, forward the buyer the pickup instructions.
5. Platform refunds you in 1–2 days (often hours).

Delay scenario (item late, buyer hasn't paid the price of waiting):
- Apologise, say there's a delivery delay, ask if they're OK waiting a few more days.
- Not OK → refund **immediately**, then claim from Shopee ("delivery took too long").
- Shopee won't refund → ask the buyer to pay when the item arrives; if they
  refuse the item entirely, arrange pickup and refund on arrival.

---

## 6. Bank-freeze prevention (why the rules above exist)

Your bank account gets frozen when a buyer reports you to the police as a scammer.
That happens in exactly two cases: an order that never ships, or a **promised date
that was missed** by a seller who went quiet. So:

- Ranges, never dates ("2–3 working days").
- Any delay → proactive apology + refund offer.
- Refund fast, claw back from Shopee after — never the other way round.
- Keep a **second bank account** with float in it. A freeze takes a police-station
  visit to undo; don't let it strand all your cash.

---

## 7. Scam check (fake CarouPay sale emails)

Phishing emails saying "you have a CarouPay sale" target new seller accounts.

- A sale is real **only if it appears in-app**: profile → My Sales tab.
- Legit Carousell email domains: `carousell.com` / `carousell.sg` only.
- Never click payment/confirmation links from email. Confirm in the app, always.
